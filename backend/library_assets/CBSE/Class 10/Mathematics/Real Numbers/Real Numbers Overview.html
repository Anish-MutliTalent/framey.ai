Key ideas

  • Euclid's Division Lemma: for any positive integers a and b, there exist unique integers q and r such that a = bq + r, 0 ≤ r < b.
  • Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic: every composite number can be expressed as a product of primes in essentially one way.
  • Revisiting irrational numbers: √2, √3, √5 are irrational.
  • Decimal expansions: terminating, non-terminating recurring, or non-terminating non-recurring — and what each says about the denominator.
